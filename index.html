Ficus

A popular, evergreen, plant with shiny, strong burgundy leaves. They are quite low maintenance when in the correct lighting and also remove chemical toxins from the air.

- Bright indirect sun and/or full sun, part shade.

- Keep soil moist but not super wet, water when soil is dry to touch. Prefers freely draining soil.

- Prefer warmer, more humid temperatures.

- Ficus sap can be toxic to dogs, so be mindful.

×

Monstera Deliciosa

Monstera is an evergreen climbing plant, using its long aerial roots to seek support and climb trees. With majestic glossy leaves that can grow up to 90cm in length when mature with thick green stems.

- Bright indirect light indoors or partially shaded outside.

- Keep moist in warmer weather, let it dry out completely between waterings.

- Being a tropical plant, It enjoys warmer temperatures, but can easily adapt to any indoor conditions.

- Mildly toxic to dogs and cats, do not consume.

×

Majesty Palm

Also known as the Neanthe bella, is one of the most sought after indoor palms, thriving in homes and workplaces with low maintenance care and a reasonable price point as opposed to other palms.

- Bright to medium indirect light.

- Can be sensitive to overwatering, prefer to be on the drier side. Water when top inch of soil is dry.

- Parlour Palms are happy in average home temperature.

- Completely pet safe!

Peace Lily

Peace Lilies are another popular office and house plant. When it comes to care, Peace lilies are one of the most low maintenance houseplants, making them perfect for plant parents of all knowledge levels.

- Prefers bright indirect light but can condition itself to medium light.

- Soak water all the way through when watering, wait till the top inch of soil is dry before watering next.

- Enjoy more humid temperatures.

- Can be mildly toxic to cats.

×

Tradescantia

The perfect plant for hanging baskets! With beautiful purple, green and silver waxy leaves and long beautiful trailing vines this easy grower is perfect for any household.

- Do best in medium to bright indirect light.

- Water well when the soil begins to dry out, keep top inch of soil moist.

- Being a tropical plant, they prefer humidity and aren’t as happy with colder conditions.

- Midly toxic to pets.

×

Alocasia

A beautiful indoor plant with large, veined leaves and long stems. This can be a challenging houseplant to care for but once you have care properly organised, it is so worth it!

- Do best in bright indirect light.

- These plants love their water, so a proper watering schedule is critical! Do not let the soil go completely dry, this may upset them and turn the dormant.

- Alocasias HATE the cold, so be sure to position it in a spot either room temperature or warmer.

- Can be toxic if consumed, be mindful.

Cactus

Cactus are succulent plants, with thick large stems and pointy spines. Some species of cactus can even produce beautiful bright flowers in the warmer months.

- Lots of bright light, but be mindful up against windows as this can magnify the light and cause burning.

- Water once a week but be sure the soil has dried out between waterings. Cut back in winter as they go dormant in colder months.

- Prefer humid conditions.

- Not toxic, but can be harmful to pets due to the sharp spines.

×

Watermelon Peperomia

These peltate leaves are oval/circle shaped with a mixture of green and silver stripes. The leaves have a fleshy feel to them and sprout out in all directions, with the potential to grow up to 12 inches tall!

- Thrive in a medium-light position but can also tolerate lower-light conditions.

- Allow the top few cms of soil to dry out between watering, if wet let it dry out further. Water around once a week, with just enough water to start to see some draining from the bottom of the pot. Reduce watering in winter when the plant isn’t in an active growing phase.

- No preferences.

- Non toxic to pets!

×

String of Pearls

String of Pearls are a creeping, succulent like vine that are perfect to display in hanging baskets or high shelves.

- Prefer bright indirect light.

- They are drought tolerant, but need a good soak every 2 weeks, water again once soil has dried out.

- Like room temperature to higher humidity.

- Can be toxic to both dogs and humans, do not consume!

5 Easy Care Plants

1. Snake Plant

Looking for that low maintenance lover as a first time plant parent? Or simply have limited time or lighting? Snake plants, also known as 'mother-in-law's tongue' are a great option for you! Doing fine in bright, medium and lower indirect light.

They can go weeks without a drink making them a great addition to any paced household or if you're a frequent traveller.

2. ZZ Plant

On the hunt for a houseplant that is virtually indestructible? Then the ZZ Plant is the perfect match for you. Tall, dark and glossy, with tall foliage that can grow up to 3ft! A stunning addition to any corner of your home.

This low maintenance beauty will happily thrive in a shady spot as well as indirect light, it is also happy to be root bound, reducing the need for regular repotting.

3. Devils Ivy

The perfect plant for those darker rooms and corners or your home.

This viney stunner is made up of lush heart shaped leaves, ideal for hanging baskets or shelving sending out long lush stems that reach up to 65 ft. It is super forgiving and ideal for forgetful waterers or those lacking in sunny living spaces. Just look at those vines!

4. Chinese Moneyplant

The Chinese money plant has beautiful bright green pancake-shaped leaves that provide an adorable and welcome burst of colour against any space.

Money plants prefer bright light, with no direct sunlight. They’re hardy to frost and are likely to put out stunning tiny white flowers on pink stems in colder temperatures.
